The lender_offers function allows bitcoin holders to offer loans of various denominations based on their bitcoin holdings using DLCs. The function works by first creating a DLC that specifies the amount of bitcoin that is being offered as a loan, the duration of the loan, and the conditions under which the loan will be released. The DLC is then inscribed with the borrower's public key. Finally, the DLC is locked into the blockchain and the transaction is returned. Once the DLC has been locked into the blockchain, the borrower can accept the loan offer by locking the specified amount of collateral. If the borrower repays the loan, they will have their collateral returned. If the borrower does not repay the loan, the collateral will be sent to the lender. Here are the steps in more detail: 1. The lender creates a DLC that specifies the amount of bitcoin that they are willing to offer as a loan, the duration of the loan, and the conditions under which the loan will be released. 1. The lender inscribes the DLC with the borrower's public key. This ensures that only the borrower can redeem the DLC. 1. The lender locks the DLC into the blockchain. This makes the loan offer binding and prevents the lender from changing their mind.1. The borrower can accept the loan offer by locking the specified amount of collateral. This collateral is held as security in case the borrower defaults on the loan.1. If the borrower repays the loan on time, they will have their collateral returned.1. If the borrower does not repay the loan, the collateral will be sent to the lender.This function provides a secure and decentralized way for bitcoin holders to offer loans to borrowers. The DLC ensures that the loan offer is binding and that the borrower cannot simply walk away from the loan without repaying it. The collateral requirement provides additional security for the lender in case the borrower defaults on the loan.
